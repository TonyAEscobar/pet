Kookaburra

Where Kookaburras Live

Kookaburras are birds that live in Australia and New Guinea, which grow between 11 and 19 inches. They are terrestrial tree kingfishers of the genus Dacelo, even though they are not associated with water. They mainly live in forests, and are carnivorous, so they eat only meat. Dispite rarely eating fish, they have sometimes stolen goldfish from garden ponds.

Kookaburra Facts

Kookaburras have calls that sound like human laughter, which can be heard from far away. Kookaburras can fly up to 20 mph, and a group of Kookaburras is called a Riot. Dispite its small size, it is the largest in the Kingfisher Family. Kookaburras can live to up to 15 years, and like the rest of the Kingfisher Family, they are some of the only birds with the ability to hover in place.
